Establishing a Business in Canada and the UK

Forming a Business in Canada: Incorporation Guide

Incorporating as a Non-Resident

  • Director Residency: As of July 2021, no longer mandates that 25% of directors be Canadian residents, allowing full foreign ownership and directorship.
  • Registered Office: A physical office address is mandatory.
  • Local Support: Non-residents may need to engage a local service provider to handle registered office requirements and compliance filings.
  • Banking Requirements: Opening a Canadian business bank account may require a physical presence for certain banking and financing needs.

Corporation Types for Global Business

  • Federal Corporation (Canada Business Corporations Act):
    • Operates across all Canadian provinces and territories.
    • Offers robust brand protection and recognition.
    • Ideal for businesses with national or global aspirations.
  • Provincial Corporation:
    • Simpler setup and compliance processes, suitable for localized operations.

Recommended for International Trade: A federal corporation or provincial corporation with a detailed import/export business plan and GST/HST registration for tax compliance.

Compliance Requirements

  • Share Capital: No minimum capital is required. Typically, 1–10,000 common shares without par value are authorized.
  • Share Classes: Multiple classes (e.g., common, preferred) with distinct rights can be issued, as specified in the Articles of Incorporation.
  • Financial Reporting: Annual financial statements are required, adhering to Canadian GAAP or IFRS. Audits are optional unless requested by shareholders.
  • Directors: At least one director is required, with no residency restrictions as of 2021.
  • Company Secretary: Not required under law.
  • Shareholders: A minimum of one shareholder (individual or corporate) is required, and non-residents can hold 100% of shares.

Setting Up a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) in the United Kingdom

A UK Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) combines the tax treatment of a partnership with the limited liability of a company. It is a flexible structure for businesses, requiring at least two designated members who assume responsibilities similar to company directors.

  • Key Responsibilities of Designated Members:
    • Appointing an auditor (if required).
    • Preparing and signing financial accounts.
    • Filing accounts with the Registrar of Companies.
    • Notifying the Registrar of changes in membership, registered office, or LLP name.
    • Submitting an annual return (Form LLP363).
    • Managing the LLP’s obligations during dissolution or winding up.

Steps to Establish a UK LLP

  • Legal Framework: Governed by the Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2000 and the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1988 (as amended).
  • Corporate documents and legislation are in English (Welsh is permitted for LLPs registered in Wales).
  • Incorporation Process:
    • Submit Form LLP2 (Incorporation Document) with the registration fee to the Registrar of Companies.
    • Provide the LLP’s name, registered office address, and details of initial members (including birthdates for individuals).
    • Specify at least two designated members responsible for compliance.
    • Incorporation typically takes 3–5 working days, subject to name approval and document certification.
  • Registered Office: Must be located in the UK.
  • Name Restrictions: Names cannot be identical or too similar to existing entities, offensive, or imply connections to the Royal Family or UK Government. Terms like "bank" or "assurance" require special approval.
  • Powers of an LLP: Equivalent to those of a natural person in the UK.
  • Shelf LLPs: Pre-registered LLPs are available upon request.
  • Suffix: Must include "Limited Liability Partnership" or "LLP."
  • People with Significant Control (PSC) Register: Since 2016, LLPs must identify and register individuals or entities with significant control (PSCs) with Companies House to ensure transparency and prevent misuse.

Compliance and Taxation

  • Capital Contribution: Minimum of £2.
  • Taxation: Members are exempt from UK tax if no business is conducted in the UK and the LLP operates as a profit-seeking entity (not in liquidation).
  • Double Taxation Treaties: The UK has extensive double tax treaty networks, but LLPs’ access depends on members’ residency, limiting treaty benefits for international LLPs.
